# v3.2.1 — AI Bestie persona fix, plainer Bestie's Take, reactive analytics

**Release date:** 2026-05-30
**Previous release:** v3.2.0
**Scope:** 3 commits · 9 files · +221 / −120

A patch release on top of v3.2.0. The headline is a correctness fix to the AI
Bestie's persona: it now consistently **represents** the woman to her matches
instead of **impersonating** her. Also includes a readability fix to the
"Bestie's Take" profile flags and a reactivity fix in the admin analytics view.

---

## 1. AI Bestie: represents her, never impersonates her (correctness fix)

**The bug.** v3.2.0 shipped a server-side Bestie auto-responder whose prompt told
the model to ghostwrite **in the woman's first-person voice** and to **deny** that
any bestie/AI was involved ("if he asks who is chatting, respond as her: it's just
me 😊"). But the chat UI labels every Bestie message **"<Owner>'s AI Bestie"** and
shows an intro card telling the match he's being helped by her bestie. The two
halves contradicted each other: the match would read the label, ask "am I talking
to her or her bestie?", and the bot would lie — directly under a label saying
otherwise.

This was a regression. The **original** (documented, 2026-05-22) design was the
correct one: *"You are NOT her. You are her bestie… refer to her in the third
person."* The v3.2.0 server-side rewrite silently inverted it, and because the
persona was retyped inline in each endpoint, nothing caught the drift.

**The fix.** The representative model is restored as the **single source of
truth**:

- **New `buildBestieReplyPrompt` in `src/lib/prompts.ts`** is the canonical
  persona for every match-facing Bestie message. Hard rules:
  - Speaks in its own voice **as the bestie**, never as the woman.
  - **Always** refers to her in the third person, by name.
  - **Honest about who it is.** If the match asks who he's talking to (or mentions
    a bestie/AI), it says plainly: he's talking to her AI bestie, helping her get
    to know matches before she jumps in herself. It never claims to be her or
    pretends a message came from her.
  - May share genuine things about her to build connection, within her boundaries
    and dealbreakers; never shares contact details or private information.
- **Both responders now build from it.** `src/lib/server/bestie-responder.ts` (the
  always-on server-side responder) and the `ai-bestie/generate-response` endpoint
  no longer carry their own duplicated impersonation prompts — they call the
  canonical builder, so the persona can't diverge again.
- **Opening message reworked.** `ai-bestie/opening-message` now introduces the
  bestie on her behalf (the owner's name is passed from the chat page) instead of
  writing a ready-to-send message in her first-person voice.
- **No UI change needed.** The "<Owner>'s AI Bestie" label, the ✨ gradient avatar,
  and the male-facing intro card were always consistent with this model — they
  stay. Only the generation prompts were wrong.

**Docs reconciled.** The v3.2.0 release note (which had codified the impersonation
model as a feature) and `docs/AI_BESTIE_FEATURE.md` are corrected to describe the
representative model and point at `buildBestieReplyPrompt` as the source of truth.

> No database migration. No API contract change. Behavior change only: match-facing
> Bestie messages now read as her bestie, in third person, and answer the
> "who am I talking to?" question honestly.

## 2. "Bestie's Take" flags read in plain English

The `POST /api/verified-vibe/bestie-profile-flags` prompt was leaking internal
field names into user-facing flag details (e.g. "totalVerifiedProofCategories is
0", "travelLocationCount is 0", "trust_score is 75"). The prompt now requires
conversational phrasing written for a female dating-app user — "no trips have been
verified", "wealth hasn't been verified", "his trust score is 75" — capped at 25
words per detail.

---

## 3. Admin analytics filters update reactively

On `/admin/analytics`, changing a filter now recomputes the KPIs and charts
reactively instead of showing stale figures until a manual refresh.
