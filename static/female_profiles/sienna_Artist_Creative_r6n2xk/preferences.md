# Dating Preferences — Sienna
**Archetype:** The Artist / Creative Idealist
**Age:** 25 | **Occupation:** Illustrator / Graphic Designer (Freelance)
**Platform:** Hinge, Tinder

---

## Who She Is
Sienna makes things — illustrations, murals, zines, weird collages that mean something to her if not immediately to you. Her life is structured around creative output and the community that surrounds it: gallery openings, studio shares, late-night conversations about meaning and form. She earns money from freelance design work and is not ashamed of the financial patchwork this sometimes creates. She is looking for someone who genuinely inhabits an inner life, not someone who nods along while waiting for the abstract part to be over.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Intellectual and creative curiosity — doesn't have to be an artist, but has to care about something beyond the functional
- Emotionally available and able to go deep in conversation
- Respects her creative work as serious, not as a hobby to be outgrown
- Not transactionally minded about relationships — interested in depth over optimization
- Can be present: sits with a painting, reads slowly, watches a film without scrolling

### Strong preferences
- Has his own creative or intellectual practice — writing, music, design, philosophy, anything
- Interesting to have conversations with at 2am that aren't about work
- Comfortable with financial imperfection — his or hers — if the life is rich in other ways
- Reads. Actually reads.
- Values the experience of beauty and strangeness — not just comfort and certainty

### Open to
- Men from very different backgrounds if the curiosity and depth are there
- Non-linear life paths — she doesn't trust conventional narratives about success
- Long-distance if the connection is real and the visits are meaningful

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Men who want her to "get a real job" eventually
- Anyone who talks about her work as charming rather than serious
- The finance guy who thinks one gallery visit makes him culturally compatible
- Relationships that require her to dim her intensity or explain her references
- People who don't have a relationship with art, books, or something that can't be monetized

---

## How She Communicates
- Communicates in layers — what she says is usually pointing at something else
- Will test intellectual depth early and lose interest quickly if it's shallow
- Long conversations: can talk for hours if the connection is real
- Poor at administrative communication: scheduling, logistics, following up on practical things
- Affection is expressed through sharing — showing you her work, giving you a book, introducing you to her world

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Has an opinion about something artistic or cultural that is actually his own, not sourced from reviews
- Can sit in a gallery for more than fifteen minutes
- Talks about a piece of music, a film, or a book the way it clearly affected him
- Makes something — anything — with his hands or his mind
- Curious about her work without making it about his appreciation of her

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- Uses "I like all kinds of music" to avoid revealing actual taste
- Comes to a show, doesn't engage, and doesn't mention it again
- His "creative side" is one photography Instagram updated three years ago
- References culture as credentials rather than as something that moved him
- Wants her to be less intense once the novelty wears off

---

## Red Flags
- Condescending about her career choice or earnings
- Thinks her work is decorative — nice, but not serious
- Fundamentally values security and certainty above all else and can't make peace with her life
- Wants her to be creative and muse-like in private but conventional in public
- Has no inner life — just a resume

---

## Notes for Interview
Sienna will engage deeply if the questions are real. She will disengage instantly if they feel like an intake form. Press on: whether the depth she presents is sustainable in a relationship or disappears when practicalities intrude, whether she can negotiate the material reality of a partnership alongside the ideal, and what she actually needs day-to-day from a person. Ask what she's working on right now and what's frustrating about it.
