# Dating Preferences — Rachel
**Archetype:** The Dog Mom / Wine / Tacos Everygirl
**Age:** 28 | **Occupation:** HR Generalist / Elementary School Teacher
**Platform:** Bumble, Tinder, Hinge

---

## Who She Is
Rachel's profile reads like a checklist of millennial dating-app signifiers because, honestly, those things are accurate: she does love her dog, she does enjoy wine with her friends, she does eat tacos whenever given the opportunity. She is not performing these preferences — she just also hasn't thought deeply about whether they communicate who she actually is. Beneath the legible surface is someone warm, steadily employed, close to her family, and genuinely ready for the relationship her profile implies. She is not cynical, not complicated, and not playing games. She is also not particularly self-examined, and that is not always a problem.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Kindness — the through-line for everything else
- Likes dogs or can genuinely tolerate one that sleeps in the bed
- Ready for something real — not "open to it" but actually pursuing it
- Family-oriented, or at minimum family-friendly
- Consistent and reliable; shows up

### Strong preferences
- Has his own solid friend group — not an island
- Enjoys social occasions without needing to leave early
- Has some fun hobbies or things he does with his weekends
- Doesn't take himself too seriously; can be silly
- Wants the same things: relationship, maybe a house someday, kids at some point

### Open to
- Different career paths — she's not filtering by income bracket
- Introverts if they're warm and willing to engage with her social world
- Long-distance short-term if there's a clear plan to be in the same place

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Situationships, "casually seeing where it goes," or emotional unavailability
- Anyone who will make her choose between him and her dog (this has happened)
- Guys who are mean to service workers
- The guy who is always the most interesting person in the room — exhausting
- People who are too good for things that are just fun

---

## How She Communicates
- Warm and open — doesn't play games with texting
- Expects relatively consistent communication; occasional check-ins matter to her
- Says what's wrong but needs to feel safe to do it — doesn't lead with conflict
- Big on celebrating things: birthdays, milestones, small wins
- Will make effort proportional to effort received — tracks reciprocity without being cynical about it

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Remembers the name of her dog within the first two conversations
- Plans things — dinner, weekend activities, even just a walk
- Gets along with her friends without trying to impress them
- Sends a "how did that thing go?" follow-up about something she mentioned
- Is genuinely just nice to be around, consistently

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- Profile is perfectly calibrated and in-person he is immediately less warm
- Can't name a single thing he's passionate about
- Very agreeable early on; she can't tell what he actually thinks
- Allergic to dogs but "willing to try" — not likely to end well
- His idea of a good weekend is very different from hers and he's not flexible

---

## Red Flags
- Dismissive of things she enjoys because they're mainstream
- Makes her feel silly for liking what she likes
- Not close to anyone — family estranged, no real friends, exes all "crazy"
- Says he wants a relationship but shows all the behavioral signs of someone who doesn't
- Prioritizes everything else over time with her once the novelty passes

---

## Notes for Interview
Rachel is one of the easier interviews — she knows what she wants, it's legible, and she'll tell you. The questions worth pressing on are: what's underneath the surface-level profile (she has more going on than her preferences list suggests), what the relationship she's been in before looked like, and whether she is as easygoing about what she gives in a relationship as she appears. She deserves more credit than the archetype suggests — find it.
