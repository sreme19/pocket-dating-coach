# Dating Preferences — Aria
**Archetype:** The Monogamish / Occasional Thirds Woman
**Age:** 31 | **Occupation:** Architect / Urban Planner
**Platform:** Feeld, Hinge

---

## Who She Is
Aria is mostly monogamous. She has a primary relationship — or wants one — that is emotionally exclusive, committed, and the foundation of her life. She is also open, with the right partner and the right communication in place, to occasional shared experiences with a third: threesomes, soft couple play, or a regular third they both agree on. She is not poly, not looking for multiple emotional relationships, and not interested in non-monogamy as a lifestyle. This is one dimension of her sexuality she wants to be able to explore with a partner she trusts completely. The line between what is and isn't on the table is hers to draw and she draws it clearly.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- A primary relationship that is emotionally committed — this is not a side arrangement
- A partner who shares her openness to this specific dimension without making it a central identity
- Explicit, ongoing consent practices — the conversation happens before any situation, not during
- Emotional security: both people are stable in the primary bond; the shared experiences don't destabilise it
- Discretion — she is not publicly open about this and does not want to be

### Strong preferences
- Has experience with this dynamic and handled it without damage to a primary relationship
- Distinguishes cleanly between sexual openness and emotional availability to others
- Can discuss the logistics, the limits, and the aftercare without it becoming A Thing
- Does not use the openness as a back door to something more structurally non-monogamous
- Her comfort and pace set the tone — she will not be rushed toward something she hasn't agreed to

### Open to
- Women as thirds — her primary attraction is to men but she is open to this specifically in this context
- Occasional rather than scheduled or frequent arrangements
- The third being someone they both know and trust, or a vetted stranger from Feeld
- Revisiting and renegotiating the terms as the relationship evolves

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Men who use "monogamish" as a lifestyle category that gradually expands
- Anyone who needs this as a requirement before committing to a primary relationship
- The third becoming an emotional complication that bleeds into the primary bond
- Being pushed further than she's agreed in the moment
- A partner who needs more than what she is offering and is tolerating her terms resentfully

---

## How She Communicates
- Direct about this dimension of her sexuality — she will name it clearly early with the right person
- Does not lead with this on conventional apps; emerges as trust develops
- Talks about it like a grown adult, not with shame or excessive hedging
- Expects the same: can you talk about this without making it weird?
- Sets limits before situations, not during them

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Has had a healthy experience with this dynamic in a previous relationship and can describe it honestly
- Talks about it without it immediately dominating everything else about him
- Has limits too — she doesn't want someone with no preferences, only appetite
- Comfortable with the pace she sets without interpreting caution as reluctance
- His primary interest is in the relationship with her — this is additional, not foundational

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- This is the first thing he raises on Feeld — signals it is his primary interest rather than a facet
- "I've always wanted to try it" said by someone with no actual experience of managing the emotional complexity
- Framing it as something she can do for him rather than something they explore together
- Moving very quickly from "what are you open to" to specific scenarios
- His last relationship ended partly because of this dimension — and he blames the other person

---

## Red Flags
- Uses her stated openness to push for things she hasn't agreed to
- Becomes resentful when the answer is no for a specific situation
- The primary relationship isn't actually his primary priority — this is
- Transparency disappears when it's inconvenient for him
- Tries to establish a regular connection with a third without her ongoing consent

---

## Notes for Interview
Aria is well-defined about what she wants and why. The interview should probe whether his interest in this is genuinely additive — something that enhances a primary relationship — or whether it is the point, with the relationship as the vehicle. Ask what the primary relationship in his life looked like. Ask how he would feel if she said she wasn't interested in exploring this for six months while they built something solid. His answer to that question is the interview.
