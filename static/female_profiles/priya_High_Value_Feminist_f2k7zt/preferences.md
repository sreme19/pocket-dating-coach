# Dating Preferences — Priya
**Archetype:** The High-Value Feminist
**Age:** 30 | **Occupation:** Tech Startup Founder / Senior Product Lead
**Platform:** Hinge, Bumble

---

## Who She Is
Priya has built a career, a clear worldview, and a life that requires nothing from a partner except presence and equality. She has read the books, done the work, and formed a coherent framework for what a relationship should look like — and she will know, quickly, whether you fit it. She is genuinely feminist, not performatively so: it shows in how she runs her team, manages money, and makes decisions. She is also, privately, someone who wants to be softened and surprised — to find a man who makes the framework feel less necessary. She has not said this out loud.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- True equality: splits things equitably, doesn't keep score, doesn't lead with chivalry as a power dynamic
- Respects her work as seriously as she does — no "so when are you going to slow down?"
- Emotionally intelligent and has done real work on himself
- Has his own views and will defend them — she does not want a yes-man
- Comfortable with her success: income, visibility, ambition

### Strong preferences
- Feminist in practice, not just in stated identity
- Doesn't need to lead or dominate to feel secure
- Values partnership over hierarchy in all dimensions of a relationship
- Can talk about gender dynamics without getting defensive
- Has close relationships with women — colleagues, family, friends — that are uncomplicated

---

### Open to
- Men who haven't used the word feminist about themselves but whose behavior earns it
- Different industries and income brackets — she's not status-matching, she's values-matching
- Long-distance if the equity is built into the structure

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- The guy who calls himself a feminist but expects her to manage the household
- Anyone who tells her she's "intimidating" and means it as a warning
- Men who treat her success as something to compete with or eventually manage
- Old-school chivalry used as a method of control
- The man who wanted a strong woman until she was stronger than him

---

## How She Communicates
- Direct, concise, and precise — she does not pad
- Will call out things that don't sit right as they happen, not two weeks later
- High standards for follow-through: says she'll do something, does it
- Not naturally effusive in early dating — warmth emerges once trust is established
- Will have the hard conversations; expects the same without it becoming a fight

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Talks about women in his life with genuine respect — peers, not accessories
- Suggests splitting the bill without it being a statement
- Has opinions that push back on hers respectfully — she will test for this
- His idea of masculine pride doesn't require her to be smaller
- Takes domestic responsibilities without being thanked for it like it's a favor

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- Performs feminism for her and drops it elsewhere
- Comfortable with her success in theory; increasingly uncomfortable with specifics
- Says "I love that you're ambitious" — men who find it remarkable haven't normalized it
- Suggests she slow down as things get more serious
- His female friends are always complicated or chaotic — pattern, not coincidence

---

## Red Flags
- Feels emasculated by her income, title, or visibility
- Uses "feminist" as an accusation during disagreements
- Expects her to adapt her life around his geography or timeline without negotiation
- Domestic defaults fall entirely to her once they're sharing space
- Supported her career goals until they actually conflicted with his comfort

---

## Notes for Interview
Priya is sharp and will see through performative answers. The questions worth pressing on are the ones about practice versus principle — where his stated values and his actual behavior diverge. Ask him to describe the last time he took on something domestic without being asked. Ask what equality in a relationship looks like to him in a week when both people are overwhelmed. Ask whether he's ever dated someone more successful than him and how it felt.
