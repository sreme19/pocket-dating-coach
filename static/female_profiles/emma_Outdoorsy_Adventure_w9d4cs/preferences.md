# Dating Preferences — Emma
**Archetype:** The Outdoorsy / Adventure Seeker
**Age:** 27 | **Occupation:** Physical Therapist / Wilderness Guide
**Platform:** Hinge, Tinder

---

## Who She Is
Emma's life is organized around being outside. She hikes, climbs, runs, kayaks, and has opinions about trail conditions and gear. Her social life is largely built around people who share this — weekend trips, group climbs, sunset runs. She is warm, physically capable, and entirely uninterested in performing femininity in the way apps expect. Her photos are real: muddy boots, summit shots, no filters. She is not a phase or a personality trait — this is who she actually is, and she needs a partner who either already lives this way or genuinely wants to.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- An active lifestyle — not gym-active, outdoors-active (or genuinely willing to become that)
- Comfortable with physical challenge — keeps up, doesn't complain, doesn't need convincing
- Adventurous in a real sense: open to plans that don't have cell service
- Honest and uncomplicated — she has low tolerance for social performance
- Independent enough to have his own interests; she doesn't want a shadow

### Strong preferences
- Has his own outdoor pursuits, or genuine curiosity about hers
- Comfortable being in nature without a plan — can sit by a fire, read a map, navigate
- Doesn't need constant stimulation — can be quiet and present
- Values experience over things; travel over furniture
- Physically expressive — affectionate, tactile, present in his body

### Open to
- Different activity specialties — runner vs. climber vs. cyclist — as long as the energy matches
- Long-distance if both can travel to make it work
- Someone city-based if he's genuinely willing to spend weekends outdoors regularly

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Men who treat hiking as a first-date novelty and never go again
- Anyone who will resent her being gone on weekend trips
- Someone who wants her to trade the mountains for brunch culture
- Men who are intimidated by her physical capability
- Relationship structures that require constant availability — she disappears for weekends on purpose

---

## How She Communicates
- Direct and low-drama; says what she means
- Not a heavy texter — more responsive on the days before and after trips
- Expresses affection through doing things together, not through words
- Will tell you what's wrong rather than going quiet — expects the same
- Doesn't need a lot of emotional processing; wants functional communication

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Suggests a hike, a climb, or a run as a date without prompting
- Has a trip planned — or a list of places he wants to go
- Can pack his own bag for a weekend without asking what to bring
- Comfortable in discomfort: a bit of rain, a long approach, a cold camp
- Doesn't try to out-alpha her on outdoors knowledge

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- "I love being outside" but his version is brunch terraces
- Comes on two trips and then starts preferring hotels
- Competitive about fitness rather than playfully motivated by it
- Can't name a trail he's done or a place he wants to go
- Treats her adventures as content rather than a way of life

---

## Red Flags
- Actively dislikes being outside; comes along and makes it miserable
- Feels threatened by her fitness, capability, or the time she spends with outdoor friends
- Expects her to cut back on trips once things get serious
- Condescending about her route choices, gear, or judgment in the field
- "Can't we just take a normal vacation?" said without irony

---

## Notes for Interview
Emma interviews quickly and directly — she doesn't have patience for vague answers. Press on: how much of his outdoor identity is performed for the dating profile versus how he actually spends weekends, whether he can hold his own on a real trip or needs hand-holding, and whether he understands the difference between outdoorsy as an aesthetic and outdoorsy as a life. Best question: "Tell me about the hardest thing you've done outside in the last year."
