# Dating Preferences — Nicole
**Archetype:** The Serial Dater / Perpetually Almost-Ready
**Age:** 32 | **Occupation:** Marketing Manager / Event Planner
**Platform:** Hinge, Tinder, Bumble

---

## Who She Is
Nicole has been dating seriously for eight years and has never made it past eighteen months with anyone. She is not unaware of this. Every relationship has a story — one that usually casts her as clear-eyed and the men as unavailable, immature, or ill-timed. The stories are often true. They are also curated. She is genuinely good at early-stage relationships: magnetic, curious, fun to be around. She is less good at the part where the projection wears off and the actual person remains. She wants commitment. She is also, privately, terrified of it.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Emotional availability — not just stated, demonstrated
- Directness about intentions from the beginning: she does not want to find out at month six he wasn't sure
- Someone who has been in at least one long-term relationship and learned from it
- Can handle her socialness — she has a large friend group and an active life
- Shows consistent effort, not just early-stage intensity

### Strong preferences
- Has his own narrative about what went wrong in past relationships — and takes some accountability
- Doesn't disappear after the first three months when things get real
- Enjoys building a shared life — not just shared experiences
- Doesn't need her to be his only source of everything
- Values commitment enough to have a conversation about it before she has to ask

### Open to
- Different timelines if intentions are clear
- Long-distance if there's a genuine plan to close the gap
- Reconnecting with someone from her past if the conditions have changed

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Anyone who "isn't ready right now" — she's heard it
- Men who are great at dating and terrible at relationships
- Being someone's situationship at 32
- The guy who says he wants commitment but has no behavioral evidence of it
- Someone who makes her feel like she is asking for too much by wanting a future

---

## How She Communicates
- Highly social and fun to talk to — can hold a conversation with anyone
- Tests with small things before trusting with big ones
- Tells the story of past relationships with precision; listen for what she leaves out
- Will stay in something past its expiry date before admitting it's over
- Needs a direct conversation to end things — will not ghost, and cannot be ghosted without real damage

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Has ended a relationship because the other person wasn't the right fit — and said so clearly
- Doesn't romanticize early-stage chaos as chemistry
- Asks about her goals, not just her interests
- Makes plans more than a week out — signals he sees a future
- Comfortable talking about what he's building toward

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- Every ex is "crazy" or "too intense" — she will eventually be that too
- "I'm just looking for something organic" — means no accountability for where it goes
- Intensity that disappears exactly at the point where real intimacy would begin
- Great at texting, bad at planning actual time together
- Uses the word "situationship" affectionately — she does not

---

## Red Flags
- Refuses to define the relationship after three months of consistent dating
- Has never been in a relationship longer than six months and has no theory about why
- Keeps her at a distance socially — she's not met any of his friends after two months
- Says he wants something serious but his behavior is entirely inconsistent with that
- Emotional unavailability disguised as mystery or being "private"

---

## Notes for Interview
Nicole is self-aware enough to know her pattern but not yet honest about her role in it. The interview should gently surface: what happens when the honeymoon phase ends, whether she has ever stayed when things got hard vs. left, and what she is actually afraid of. Ask her about the last relationship she ended and why — and then ask what she would have done differently if she were being completely honest.
