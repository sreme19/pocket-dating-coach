# Dating Preferences — Deepa
**Archetype:** The Older Dater Under Time Pressure
**Age:** 33 | **Occupation:** Senior Manager, FMCG / Former Academic
**Platform:** Shaadi.com, Hinge

---

## Who She Is
Deepa spent her late twenties building a career and assuming the relationship would follow naturally. It didn't, not in the form she expected. She is now in a position that is extremely common and rarely discussed openly: she wants children, she is running out of the time she thought she had, and the pressure this creates has started to distort every relationship before it gets a chance to breathe. She is aware of this. She has named it in therapy. She cannot entirely stop doing it. She is also — beneath the urgency — a thoughtful, accomplished person who would be excellent company in a relationship that wasn't haunted by the clock.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Wants children and is willing to move toward that on a clear timeline
- Understands that she is serious about commitment — this is not ambiguity, it is context
- Emotionally ready to be a partner and eventually a parent — not still figuring out if he wants either
- Will not make her manage the relationship timeline alone
- Actually compatible with her life and values, not just suitable on paper

### Strong preferences
- Has some sense of what a family looks like for him — has thought about it beyond a vague yes
- Settled enough in his career that a child is possible in three to four years
- Tolerates or ideally understands the pressure she's under without using it against her
- Doesn't pathologize her urgency as desperation — it is biology, clarity, and honesty
- Family-adjacent if not fully traditional — understands that parenting is not a solo project

### Open to
- Divorced men with or without children if the situation is well-managed
- Age ranges wider than conventional — older is fine, younger is fine if maturity is there
- Different cultural backgrounds if children and family are shared values
- Adoption or assisted reproduction as part of the conversation

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Men who are "open to children eventually" and mean nothing concrete by it
- Anyone who will use her timeline as leverage
- The man who genuinely does not want children but thinks he can change on her schedule
- Men who interpret her seriousness as a warning about her character
- Ambiguity about intent: she cannot afford it and will not pretend otherwise

---

## How She Communicates
- More direct than most — the clock has clarified her communication
- Will name what she's looking for early; it is not a test, it is information
- Can seem intense in early conversations because the stakes are real to her
- Warms considerably once there's evidence of mutual intent — becomes genuinely relaxed and funny
- Will not ghost or fade; expects the same directness in return

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Has thought about fatherhood specifically, not just parenthood abstractly
- Doesn't flinch when the children conversation comes up
- Has his own sense of urgency or reason to be serious — not just accommodating hers
- Takes the relationship at a pace that is faster than apps-casual but not panicked
- Can be light and present, not only strategic — she needs relief from the weight too

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- "I definitely want kids, just not for a while" when she asks what a while means
- Says all the right things in month one; gets vague in month two
- Treats her seriousness as the issue rather than his own lack of clarity
- Great on paper but every conversation reveals another year he needs
- The urgency he senses from her makes him move slower, not faster

---

## Red Flags
- Uses the timeline pressure as justification for bad behavior: "I thought you'd put up with it"
- Definitively ambivalent about children but too conflict-averse to say so
- Has a pattern of dating women her age and leaving just before the commitment question
- His readiness is entirely contingent on things outside her control
- Treats children as a theoretical future item rather than something he's examined

---

## Notes for Interview
Deepa is one of the most important interviews to get right. The pressure she's under is real and it deserves respect, not pathologizing. The key questions are: whether his stated willingness to have children is genuine or appeasing, whether he understands the specific timeline she's working with, and — most importantly — whether he is actually compatible with her as a person once you look past the logistics. Ask what he imagines doing on a Tuesday night five years from now. Ask what he's afraid of about becoming a parent.
