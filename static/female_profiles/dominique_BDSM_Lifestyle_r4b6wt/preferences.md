# Dating Preferences — Dominique
**Archetype:** The BDSM-Lifestyle / Kink-Oriented Woman
**Age:** 27 | **Occupation:** Nurse / Nightlife Events Producer
**Platform:** Feeld, FetLife

---

## Who She Is
Dominique lives a double life in the most benign sense: professional, competent, and trusted by day; a fully self-aware participant in BDSM and kink communities by night. She has been part of the lifestyle for five years, has a working understanding of risk-aware consent, and knows who she is inside a dynamic. She is a submissive — she chooses it consciously, from a position of strength. She is not looking for someone to fix her, understand her as a project, or step carefully around her like she is fragile. She needs someone who knows the difference between dominance and cruelty and has practised the former.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- BDSM literacy — safe words, negotiation, aftercare, the full stack
- Someone who identifies and practises dominance consensually: structured, consistent, not impulsive
- Explicit negotiation before any new dynamic or scene — she will not improvise this with a stranger
- Emotional safety: can separate the power dynamic inside a scene from how they treat each other outside one
- Discretion — her professional life is not compatible with her private life being public

### Strong preferences
- Experience in the community: attended munches, knows the culture, not purely self-taught via internet
- Can hold a dynamic consistently — not just during scenes but in the texture of the relationship
- Understands hard and soft limits and does not probe them without negotiation
- Aftercare fluency — knows what she needs without her having to explain it every time
- Has references or standing in the community — she checks

### Open to
- Dominant men newer to lifestyle if they approach it with serious study and humility
- Switches if their dominant side is genuinely present and not just theoretical
- D/s dynamic within an otherwise conventional relationship
- Long-distance D/s with clear protocols if the emotional connection is real

## What She's Not Looking For
- Men who confuse dominance with aggression, control, or disrespect outside a negotiated scene
- "I'm naturally dominant" said by someone with no actual practice
- Anyone who uses kink as a workaround for poor communication
- Emotional unavailability as a personality trait dressed up as stoicism
- Men who need her submission to feel powerful — she is a conscious participant, not a fix for insecurity

## How She Communicates
- Precise about what she wants and needs within a dynamic — she has language for it
- Will not scene with someone she hasn't negotiated with in vanilla conversation first

## Green Flags in a Match
- Discusses negotiation and consent without making it clinical or performative
- Knows what aftercare means and can describe his approach to it unprompted
- Has his own FetLife or community standing — verifiable
- Can be a normal human being outside a scene without reverting to dom-as-personality
- Handles her professional life with respect — understands compartmentalisation without making her feel shameful

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- Has "always been this way" without any actual training or community engagement
- The dynamic is consistent only when he feels like it — she needs reliability, not mood
- Talks about BDSM as if it is primarily about sex, not structure
- Wants to push limits without the conversation that earns that right
- FetLife profile is six months old and has no community activity

---

## Red Flags
- Violates negotiated limits and explains it away as "going with the moment"
- Uses the power dynamic outside of agreed parameters to control her real life
- Has had multiple partners describe him as unsafe
- Neglects aftercare — the scene ends and he disappears emotionally
- Treats her submission as ownership

## Notes for Interview
Dominique will be calm and direct. The interview should establish his actual community standing and practice, not just stated identity. Ask for a specific scenario he has navigated where a limit was approached and what he did. Ask how he handles a scene that goes somewhere neither person planned. Ask what aftercare looks like to him after a heavy scene. The answers reveal whether this is identity or practice.
