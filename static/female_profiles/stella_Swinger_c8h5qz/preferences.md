# Dating Preferences — Stella
**Archetype:** The Swinger / Couples-Play Oriented Woman
**Age:** 34 | **Occupation:** Interior Designer / Business Co-Owner
**Platform:** Feeld, SDC, Kasidie

---

## Who She Is
Stella is in a long-term open relationship with her partner of seven years. They arrived at the swinging lifestyle deliberately, after a long conversation and a clear set of rules they have revised twice since. She is not looking to leave her partner, not looking for emotional entanglement, and not looking for men who conflate sexual openness with emotional availability. She is looking for vetted, respectful, interesting people — as a couple or separately, depending on the arrangement. She and her partner are primary; everything else is additional.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Full awareness that she is partnered and this is couples-adjacent or separately negotiated
- Discretion — she runs a business; this is a private area of her life
- Respectful, clean interaction: no unsolicited photos, no pressure after initial contact
- Either has lifestyle experience or comes in with genuine humility and is open to being guided
- Clear about what he is looking for — solo male, couple, separate arrangement with her

### Strong preferences
- Has been in the lifestyle — attends events, has vetted connections, understands etiquette
- Can have a real conversation that isn't a preamble to sexual negotiation
- Physically looks after himself — health-aware, gets regular testing
- Doesn't conflate play with ownership — fun ends, everyone goes home
- Can socialise warmly as a regular person; not weird about it the next time they meet at an event

### Open to
- Couples for full swap or soft swap depending on connection
- Solo males with references from within their community
- Separate arrangements from her partner if boundaries are clearly set
- Travel for events — she and her partner attend lifestyle events abroad periodically

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Lifestyle "curious" couples where one partner is not actually on board
- Single men who want to be the emotional third of a troubled relationship
- Anyone who treats her as a stepping stone to her partner or vice versa
- Men who become attached and then resent the limits of what this is
- Oversharing personal life outside of the lifestyle context — she keeps these worlds cleanly separate

---

## How She Communicates
- Professional and low-drama — she is not here to process or be processed
- Clear about terms upfront; expects the same
- Will cut contact without explanation if boundaries are crossed
- Warm and social at events — this is actually fun for her, not a transaction
- Will not engage with anyone who hasn't passed a basic conversation test

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Has a couple or community reference she can verify
- Communicates about STI status and testing without being asked
- Reads the room at events — doesn't push, doesn't hover
- Can engage with her partner as a full human, not just as an obstacle or side character
- Knows the difference between a soft no and an actual no

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- Claims lifestyle experience but knows none of the community language or etiquette
- Responds to "we" as if her partner doesn't exist and he only wants her
- Moving very fast — pressure in this space is always a warning
- Won't provide testing status
- One half of a couple is clearly more enthusiastic than the other

---

## Red Flags
- Violates agreed terms during play — any kind of boundary breach
- Contacts her outside of the agreed platform or schedule without permission
- Discloses the connection to anyone in her professional or social world
- Tries to develop emotional dependency she hasn't invited
- His idea of discretion is "I won't post photos" — not the standard she operates at

---

## Notes for Interview
Stella is one of the most efficiently boundaried people in this library. The questions to press on are: how he handles the end of an arrangement when feelings have developed on one side, whether he understands the couple hierarchy and is actually comfortable with it, and whether his lifestyle experience is genuine or aspirational. Ask what happens when a play partner wants more and how he has handled that before.
