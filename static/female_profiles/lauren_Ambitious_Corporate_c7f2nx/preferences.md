# Dating Preferences — Lauren
**Archetype:** The Ambitious Corporate Woman
**Age:** 29 | **Occupation:** Strategy Consultant (Big 4)
**Platform:** Hinge, Bumble

---

## Who She Is
Lauren has built exactly the life she planned: target school, fast-track career, her own apartment in a city she chose. She is smart, capable, and privately exhausted by a work culture that demands everything. She comes to dating with high standards and a low tolerance for ambiguity — both features of the same mind that makes her excellent at her job. She wants a relationship that adds to her life, not one she has to manage like another project.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Intellectual equals or higher — she needs to respect you to be attracted to you
- Ambition with direction — not just a title, but someone who is genuinely building something
- Emotional maturity: can handle her directness without wilting or retaliating
- Someone who takes initiative — plans dates, makes decisions, doesn't ask "so what do you want to do?"
- Honest communication — she will not chase subtext

### Strong preferences
- Values her career without making it a competition or a novelty
- Has his own full life; doesn't need her attention to function
- Able to be vulnerable without a performance of vulnerability
- Can match her socially — comfortable in professional settings, interesting at dinner

### Open to
- Different industries or income brackets — provided there's ambition and direction
- Long-distance short-term if trajectory is clear
- Someone quieter or more introverted than her social persona suggests

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Men who need constant reassurance or emotional maintenance
- Anyone who treats her career as a quirk to be tolerated or a threat to manage
- Passive men who wait for her to lead everything then resent her for it
- "I'm just seeing what's out there" — she doesn't have time for that
- A relationship that requires her to shrink

---

## How She Communicates
- Direct and efficient — says what she means
- Texts in paragraphs or not at all; erratic response time is work, not disinterest
- Will cancel plans for a genuine work emergency — this happens, it's not a test
- Shows interest through follow-up: remembers what you said three conversations ago
- Softens significantly once trust is established — the warmth is real, it just takes time to access

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Has opinions he'll defend respectfully — doesn't just agree with everything she says
- Makes a plan and executes without needing to be managed
- Asks about her work with genuine curiosity, not performative interest
- Has close friendships and a life she's being invited into, not one built around her
- Handles her cancelling plans gracefully — once

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- Brings up her salary or his in ways that feel like positioning
- Agrees with everything she says — she'll lose respect quickly
- Sends three texts in a row when she hasn't responded — she's at work, not ignoring him
- Describes himself as "laid-back" — code for passive
- "I love a woman who knows what she wants" — often said by men who actually don't

---

## Red Flags
- Feels threatened by her income, title, or travel schedule
- Makes her career the punchline of a joke more than once
- Needs her to manage his emotions while also managing her own career and life
- Has dated a series of women he describes as "too intense" or "too career-focused"
- Competes with her rather than complementing her

---

## Notes for Interview
Lauren interviews well — she's articulate, direct, and knows what she wants. The questions to press on are the ones she gives smooth, practised answers to: what she actually needs emotionally (she tends to understate this) and how much space she genuinely has in her life for a relationship right now. She is more traditional in private than her professional identity suggests — probe for this.
