# Dating Preferences — Kavya
**Archetype:** The Modern-But-Family-First Professional
**Age:** 30 | **Occupation:** Product Manager (Bangalore-based tech company)
**Platform:** Shaadi.com, Hinge

---

## Who She Is
Kavya has the clearest self-description of any archetype in this cohort: she is a modern woman who has never pretended the family dimension is optional. She wants a career and a family. She wants equality in the partnership and she also expects certain traditional structures to remain in place — especially around families of origin and the eventual geography of the household. She is not confused about this. She is only confused by men who say they want the same thing and then reveal, piece by piece, that they want either a traditional wife or a fully independent partner — not her specific and considered hybrid.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Career-oriented himself but genuinely supportive of hers — not just tolerant
- Family-oriented: parents are important to her and must be to him as well
- Bangalore-based or willing to move there — she is not relocating internationally
- Intent to marry within a clear timeframe: she is not doing the indefinite courtship
- Can hold both ambition and family as values simultaneously, without one being the concession

### Strong preferences
- Parents who are warm and manageable — not absent, not intrusive
- Can navigate the intersection of modern dating and family involvement without embarrassment
- Builds toward a shared future that includes both of their families, not just his
- Values her professional identity and doesn't expect her to step back unless she chooses
- Has a genuine opinion about what kind of home and family life he wants — not vague

### Open to
- Men from other cities if the relocation question is genuinely open
- Different regional backgrounds within India if cultural values broadly align
- International education backgrounds that haven't entirely deracinated his Indian grounding

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Men who want her to slow down professionally once married
- Anyone who uses "family-first" to mean only his family
- Fully Westernized men who find family involvement suffocating
- Men who have never thought about what they want from family life
- Situationship culture imported into a Shaadi.com context

---

## How She Communicates
- Direct and efficient — she does not traffic in hints
- Family contact during courtship is expected and welcomed; she is not embarrassed by it
- Will say clearly what she needs from a partner if asked — she has thought about it
- Expresses warmth through consistency and follow-through, not effusion
- Will raise concerns early rather than accumulate them silently

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Can describe specifically what he wants family life to look like — not in platitudes
- His relationship with his parents is warm and functional, not codependent or estranged
- Has a professional peer relationship with women at work — she will notice how he talks about them
- Makes a plan for the next meeting, not just a vague "let's catch up again"
- Treats her time constraints as legitimate, not obstacles to romance

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- "Family is important to me" said three times without a single specific
- Progressive professionally but drops traditional assumptions at home without noticing
- Has never thought about parental involvement in the household post-marriage
- Bangalore is his fallback city, not his actual preference — she will figure this out
- Wants her professional ambition as a feature of dating and expects it to diminish as a feature of marriage

---

## Red Flags
- His family expects to be the dominant family unit; hers is expected to adjust
- Career conversations get smaller once they're more serious
- Expects a geographic move from her without equivalent consideration
- "Modern" in self-presentation but the domestic default is entirely traditional
- Can't name a single thing he would sacrifice for her career or her family relationships

---

## Notes for Interview
Kavya will articulate her preferences with unusual precision — she has a clear framework. The interview should probe for concrete behavioral evidence behind the stated values: ask what happened in his last relationship when career and family both needed something from him at the same time. Ask what his parents expect the first year of marriage to look like. Ask whether there's anything about her life in Bangalore that he would want to change.
