# Dating Preferences — Claire
**Archetype:** The Recently Divorced / Out of Long-Term Relationship
**Age:** 38 | **Occupation:** Project Manager / Operations Director
**Platform:** Hinge, Bumble

---

## Who She Is
Claire spent eleven years in a marriage that looked good from the outside and felt increasingly hollow from the inside. She is not bitter — she is clear-eyed. The divorce was her decision, or mostly hers, and she has done the processing: therapy, time, honest conversations with herself about what she let go of and what she wasn't willing to. She comes to dating with more self-knowledge than most people half her age, and a wariness that is proportional to her experience. She knows exactly what she ignored the first time. She is not going to ignore it again.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Emotional honesty — she has spent enough years with someone who couldn't do it
- A man who is genuinely available, not technically single
- Patience: she moves slower than apps expect and faster than her last relationship allowed
- Someone who can hold complexity — her life has history, kids possibly, and she does not apologize for any of it
- Long-term orientation: she is not interested in casual

### Strong preferences
- Has been through something — loss, failure, or major life transition — and come out knowing himself better
- Doesn't need the relationship to be easy to value it
- Respects her time as a finite resource — she doesn't have the hours to waste
- Financially stable and emotionally independent — she managed someone else's instability for a decade
- Can handle the fact that she has opinions, a life, and a past without being threatened

### Open to
- Divorced men who have genuinely processed their experience
- Age gaps in either direction — maturity matters more than date of birth
- Taking it slow for longer than feels natural to apps-era dating
- Blended family situations if the communication is strong

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Anyone who is technically separated but functionally still married
- Men who treat her divorce as a liability or something to manage
- Emotional unavailability dressed up as "just not rushing things"
- Anyone who wants her to be less experienced or less formed than she is
- The rebound — she knows what that looks like and will not be it, and will not let it happen to her

---

## How She Communicates
- Direct and honest — the politeness she extended for eleven years is no longer unconditional
- Will not chase: if you're interested, be interested visibly
- Texts efficiently; not available for all-day messaging
- Will end things clearly and kindly if it isn't working — expects the same
- Takes things at face value now — she stopped reading tea leaves after the divorce

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Has a clear understanding of what his marriage or long relationship taught him
- Doesn't pathologize her for leaving — takes commitment seriously without treating divorce as failure
- Has close relationships: family, long-standing friendships, something that shows he's capable of sustained connection
- Can plan something and follow through without reminders
- Treats her kids (if relevant) as part of the package without over-performing enthusiasm

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- "I just need to figure a few things out still" — code for not actually ready
- The divorce was finalized recently and he hasn't given himself time
- Makes the conversation about her divorce more than his own experience
- Has no accountability for his role in his own relationship ending
- Tries to move faster than her out of enthusiasm — often a red flag for unavailability elsewhere

---

## Red Flags
- Still emotionally entangled with his ex — talks about her constantly, negatively or otherwise
- Doesn't want to meet her children on a timeline that suits her
- Dismisses her concerns as being "baggage" from her marriage
- Has never had a relationship that lasted more than a year and is 38+
- Wants her stability and presence but isn't willing to reciprocate with real commitment

---

## Notes for Interview
Claire is one of the most honest interviewees you will encounter — she has run her own post-mortem and knows the findings. The questions worth pressing on are: what she is actually willing to compromise on now versus in her twenties, whether she has closed off more than she thinks she has, and how much of her wariness is wisdom versus a wall. Ask her what she would have wanted someone to ask her in year three of her marriage, and whether she would have answered honestly.
