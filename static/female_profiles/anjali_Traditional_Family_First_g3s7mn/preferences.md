# Dating Preferences — Anjali
**Archetype:** The Traditional Family-First Woman
**Age:** 25 | **Occupation:** Pharmacist / Recently Completed Postgrad
**Platform:** Shaadi.com

---

## Who She Is
Anjali has always known she wanted a traditional family — not as a default she fell into, but as an active preference she has thought through. She is educated and capable, and she has made a genuine choice: career is secondary to home, family comes first, and the right partner is one who understands and values this. She is not passive or without opinions, but she will express them within the structures of a traditional partnership rather than against them. She is on Shaadi.com because matrimony is the intent, not because she couldn't use Hinge.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Intent to marry — ideally within a year of serious courtship
- Family approval: her parents' opinion is not an obstacle, it is part of the process
- A man who values the traditional framework — she is not interested in converting anyone
- Same or compatible cultural/religious background (Hindu / South Indian preference)
- Wants children and wants to be the primary caregiver for at least the early years

### Strong preferences
- Settled in his career and able to provide stability
- Family-oriented: close to his own family and looking to build one
- Lives in or is willing to relocate to the same city
- Respects her education but doesn't expect her career to come first
- Traditional in values without being regressive — she still has views, opinions, and preferences

### Open to
- Career continuation post-children if the arrangement works for both
- Different states or regions within India if the match is strong
- NRI profiles if they're genuinely rooted and not straddling two cultures indefinitely

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Men who want a "modern relationship" — she is specific about what she wants
- Anyone who treats her family involvement as an inconvenience
- Partners who are on Shaadi.com but are actually looking for something casual
- Men who expect her to abandon tradition privately while performing it publicly
- Long courtship timelines with no intention to commit

---

## How She Communicates
- Measured and considered — will not overshare early
- Family is often present in early meetings; this is not a yellow flag, it is the design
- Will express preferences clearly when asked directly; does not volunteer them unprompted early
- Communication via parents is normal in this process — she is not sidelining herself by allowing it
- Softens considerably once trust is established and the match feels real

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Family is involved and positive — mutual family alignment is the foundation
- Clear timeline for marriage and children
- Stable employment; demonstrates provider capability without being asked
- Shows respect for her opinions and choices within a traditional framework
- His mother likes her and vice versa — this is considered a signal, not a pressure point

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- Technically on Shaadi.com but privately wants something much more liberal
- Expects traditional home contributions from her without offering traditional financial provision
- Family is "close" but chaotic or unsupportive of his decisions
- Vague about timeline — "whenever it feels right" is not an answer here
- Educated abroad and returned with values that don't actually align with what he's presented here

---

## Red Flags
- Expecting her to abandon her own family's proximity and expectations post-marriage
- On Shaadi.com because family pressured him; not genuinely ready
- Traditional in expectation of her but liberal in expectation of himself
- No relationship with his own family or estranged without explanation
- Views her preference for traditional family life as a power dynamic rather than a genuine value

---

## Notes for Interview
Anjali will be careful and measured early. The questions worth pressing on are what she actually wants within her traditional framework — she has opinions, preferences, and ambitions that don't disappear because she's made this choice. Ask what she hopes her life looks like at 35. Ask what she would not be willing to compromise even within a traditional marriage. Ask how she would handle a significant disagreement with her in-laws.
