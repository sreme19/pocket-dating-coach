# Dating Preferences — Luna
**Archetype:** The Recently Spiritually Awakened
**Age:** 30 | **Occupation:** Yoga Instructor / Former Marketing Associate
**Platform:** Hinge, Bumble

---

## Who She Is
Luna had a period — a breakup, a burnout, or both — that cracked something open. She came out the other side with a different relationship to time, ambition, and what she wants from people. The spiritual practice is real: daily meditation, retreats, an honest renegotiation of her values. The difficulty is that the transformation is recent enough that she is still in the story of it, and she brings that story into every new connection. She wants a partner who understands a different register of living — slower, more intentional, less relentlessly productive — but her framework is not yet stable enough to survive a partner who challenges it.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Openness to inner growth — doesn't have to practice, but must not be closed
- Present and genuinely attentive: the phone-down, eye-contact kind of presence
- Values something other than output and achievement
- Not in a relationship with hustle culture — she left that and can smell it
- Honest about what he's going through, not just what he's accomplished

### Strong preferences
- Has some relationship with stillness — meditation, nature, creative practice, whatever form it takes
- Asks questions about meaning, not just logistics
- Can sit with ambiguity without needing to resolve it
- Values health in a broad sense — body, mind, rest, connection
- Not performatively spiritual — the real kind, even if informal or unnamed

### Open to
- Men who are curious but untrained in any specific practice
- Different backgrounds if the energy is calm and real
- Non-linear careers or unconventional life structures — she's living one

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- High-achiever types whose identity is entirely their output
- Anyone who will treat her spiritual practice as a phase to be grown out of
- Men who confuse calmness with passivity — she is not a doormat with good posture
- The guy who did one Vipassana and thinks he's arrived
- Relationships that exist at the pace of productivity rather than the pace of connection

---

## How She Communicates
- Slow and thoughtful — not impulsive, in person or in text
- Will not argue; will withdraw, return, and attempt to understand
- Asks how you feel about things, not just what you think about them
- Expresses affection through space and attention, not constant contact
- Needs silences to be comfortable — does not fill every gap with words

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Has a morning practice — any kind, as long as it's his own
- Can talk about what he's let go of, not only what he's built
- Comfortable in nature; can sit by water or on a mountain without performing
- Has read something that changed how he moves through the world
- Doesn't need to be the smartest or most interesting person; just genuinely interested

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- "I'm spiritual but not religious" said to be compatible with her, not because he means it
- His idea of presence is phone on silent but still checking it every ten minutes
- Recently got into meditation and is still in the proselytizing phase
- Calm on the surface; avoidant underneath — she will eventually realize this
- Her practice makes him quietly insecure because he doesn't have one

---

## Red Flags
- Treats her career change as a mistake she'll correct eventually
- Uses her spirituality against her in conflict: "I thought you were supposed to be calm"
- Fundamentally materialist worldview and sees her differently-structured life as a problem to fix
- Performs openness to spirituality while finding it ridiculous in private
- High cortisol energy disguised as passion — she will absorb it and call it her own anxiety

---

## Notes for Interview
Luna is warm and open in conversation, which can mask how particular her requirements actually are. The questions worth pressing on are: whether his stated openness to a different pace of life is genuine or accommodation, whether he has actually examined his relationship to ambition and productivity, and whether there is a stable real person underneath the spiritual vocabulary she uses or whether the framework is still doing the work of holding her together. Ask what he would give up to live more intentionally. Ask what he stopped caring about that used to matter to him.
