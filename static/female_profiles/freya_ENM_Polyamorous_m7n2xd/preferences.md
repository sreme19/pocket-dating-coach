# Dating Preferences — Freya
**Archetype:** The Ethically Non-Monogamous / Polyamorous Woman
**Age:** 29 | **Occupation:** UX Designer / Community Organiser
**Platform:** Feeld, Hinge, OkCupid

---

## Who She Is
Freya has been practising ethical non-monogamy for four years and it is not a phase, an experiment, or a phase of her life she will conclude. She has a nesting partner she has been with for three years, one long-distance connection she sees monthly, and is actively looking for a third relationship with its own shape. She has done more thinking about consent, communication, and relationship structure than most people in ten conventional relationships combined. She is not poly because she can't commit — she is poly because she has thought about what she wants and built toward it.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Full understanding and acceptance of non-monogamy — she is not available to be the transition
- Clear communication about his relationship structure: solo poly, hierarchical, relationship anarchy, etc.
- Compersion is possible for him — not jealousy management, actual comfort with her having other partners
- Consent practice in all forms: enthusiastic, ongoing, renegotiable
- Integrity: does what he says, says what he does

### Strong preferences
- Experience with ENM — not brand new to it, not freshly curious after reading one book
- Knows his attachment style and how it operates under stress
- Has his own relationship network or at least a clear sense of what he wants
- Comfortable with scheduling, calendar transparency, metamour awareness
- Doesn't treat her other partners as threats or competitors

### Open to
- Monogamous people who are genuinely poly-curious and willing to do the reading
- Different relationship structures within ENM: solo poly, kitchen table, parallel
- Relationship anarchists if they are consistent with their principles
- Long-distance connections as a second or third relationship

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Couples looking for a unicorn — she is not a fantasy accessory
- Men who call themselves ENM because their last girlfriend agreed to let them cheat
- The jealousy that declares itself compersion
- Anyone who secretly expects her to "choose" him eventually
- "I'm open to it" said by someone who has never actually examined whether he is

---

## How She Communicates
- Thorough and explicit — she will discuss relationship structure before a second date
- Shares a calendar; expects awareness of who she's seeing and when, without surveillance
- Names feelings and expects them to be named back
- Has a check-in practice with her nesting partner — may reschedule around it
- Not defensive about her structure; will educate if asked genuinely, disengage if asked provocatively

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Can describe his current relationship structure accurately and without euphemism
- Has read something real about ENM — not just watched a documentary
- Knows what his limits are and can say them without framing them as deal-breakers about her
- Has had a difficult conversation with a partner about jealousy and learned from it
- Treats her other partners with baseline human dignity — doesn't need to like them

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- "I've always been naturally non-monogamous" without any reflection on what that has cost other people
- In the discovery phase post-breakup — may be using ENM as a wound, not a value
- Can say compersion; has never actually felt it
- His "openness" disappears when she mentions a specific partner by name
- Very good at poly theory; under-resourced when it produces actual feelings

---

## Red Flags
- Expects hierarchy with himself at the top without having negotiated it
- Wants her to reduce time with other partners because of his discomfort
- Has had multiple ENM attempts end because "the other person couldn't handle it" — he's the common variable
- Uses "freedom" to mean his freedom; her connections register as problems
- Concealing another partner from her while pursuing her

---

## Notes for Interview
Freya is articulate and has a framework. Press on the parts that are harder: whether he can genuinely handle being scheduled around, whether he has met a metamour and been okay, and whether his stated openness has been tested by an actual situation that felt bad. Ask him to describe a moment when non-monogamy produced a genuine negative feeling for him and what he did with it. That answer is the real interview.
