# Dating Preferences — Zara
**Archetype:** The Soft Life Seeker
**Age:** 26 | **Occupation:** Content Creator / Brand Partnerships
**Platform:** Hinge, Instagram DMs

---

## Who She Is
Zara has curated her life around aesthetics, ease, and intentional comfort. She works hard — content creation is a real business she runs — but the narrative she's built around herself centers on softness, abundance, and not struggling. She wants a relationship that matches that energy: someone who plans things, pays, and makes her feel taken care of without her having to ask. She is not lazy or entitled in her own framing — she is "high-value." The distinction matters to her and should matter to you.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- A provider energy: takes the lead on planning and paying, especially early on
- Emotionally present and attentive — she needs to feel chosen, visibly
- Stable income with room to grow — not necessarily rich, but not "figuring it out"
- Treats her femininity as something to be appreciated, not managed
- Someone with taste — good restaurants, thoughtful gifts, effort in presentation

### Strong preferences
- Older or established enough to be settled in his life
- Communicates consistently — good morning texts, follows up after dates
- Wants to build something — a home, a lifestyle, a family eventually
- Doesn't split bills in the early stages; understands what that signals
- Values softness in a partner and doesn't want her to be "strong and independent"

### Open to
- Men from different backgrounds if the energy and intentions are right
- Long-distance short-term if he's clearly working toward the same city
- Different industries — creative, finance, tech — provided there's stability

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- "Let's split it" — at least not in the first three months
- Men who want an equal financial partner but expect a traditionally feminine girlfriend
- Anyone emotionally unavailable or inconsistent with communication
- The guy who says "I'm not really a planner" — she is, and she will resent carrying that
- Men who treat the soft life aesthetic as shallow rather than a real value system

---

## How She Communicates
- Responds quickly when interested; goes quiet when not
- Expresses needs indirectly at first — will hint before asking directly
- Takes effort seriously: a well-planned date means more than expensive things
- Doesn't argue — withdraws, then resurfaces calmer or doesn't resurface at all
- Posts frequently; her social presence is her work and her identity — don't make her feel guilty about it

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Plans and executes dates without being asked — chooses the restaurant, makes reservations
- Compliments her appearance genuinely, not performatively
- Has a home that reflects intention — she notices
- Mentions future things casually ("we should go there sometime") without it being a pressure
- Treats her time as valuable and shows up on time

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- Makes a comment about her not having a "real job" — she earns real money
- Wants to split everything equally from date one
- Can't articulate what he's building toward in life
- Too much texting banter, not enough actual plans made
- Calls her high-maintenance — she prefers "high standards"

---

## Red Flags
- Broke or unstable and expects her to be unbothered by it
- Sees her content creation as an invitation for commentary on her appearance
- Financially stable but treats generosity as weakness
- Pushes for equality in the relationship while expecting traditional femininity from her
- Insecure about her male followers or brand partnerships

---

## Notes for Interview
Zara is warm and articulate about what she wants — she's thought about it deeply and can defend it. The questions to press on are: what she actually contributes to a relationship beyond her presence and aesthetics, whether her expectations are calibrated to her effort, and how she handles conflict when withdrawing isn't an option. She is more capable of reciprocity than her stated preferences suggest — probe for what she gives, not just what she needs.
