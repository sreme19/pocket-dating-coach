# Dating Preferences — Jade
**Archetype:** The Sugar Baby / Arrangement Seeker
**Age:** 22 | **Occupation:** Undergraduate Student (Finance / Pre-law)
**Platform:** Seeking Arrangement, Feeld, Instagram DMs

---

## Who She Is
Jade is working on a degree, managing rent in a city that prices out students, and has made a clear-eyed decision that the transactional dimension of sugar dating suits her better than pretending it isn't there in conventional dating. She is not naive — she has thought through the power dynamics, set her own terms, and entered arrangements with defined expectations on both sides. She is warm, bright, and better at reading people than most men who meet her expect. She is not available to be rescued, reformed, or made to feel shame about how she funds her life.

---

## What She's Looking For

### Non-negotiables
- Transparency about what the arrangement is — she will not pretend it is purely romantic
- An allowance or financial support that is agreed upfront and delivered consistently
- Respect: she is not an escort; the company is genuine, the terms are transactional, these coexist
- Discretion — her academic and personal life are separate from this
- Someone who is clear that she has a life outside of him and does not attempt to monopolize it

### Strong preferences
- Established, financially stable, and used to this dynamic — not experimenting
- Interesting to actually spend time with: she does not want to sit through boring dinners
- No pressure on exclusivity unless it is negotiated with equivalent financial compensation
- Does not need her to perform emotions she does not feel
- Travel is a significant plus — she values experiences over things

### Open to
- Older men (35–60+) who understand the dynamic and have run it cleanly before
- Non-sexual arrangements if the terms are generous enough
- Evolving into something more genuine if conditions naturally develop that way
- Multiple arrangements simultaneously, provided they do not overlap in her life

---

## What She's Not Looking For
- Men who want the arrangement but feel guilty about paying and punish her for it
- Anyone who expects emotional exclusivity without the financial equivalent
- Attempts to renegotiate terms once she is attached
- Treating her as a secret to the point where she feels disposable
- "I don't really like labels" said by someone who wants all the benefits and none of the accountability

---

## How She Communicates
- Direct about logistics — she asks what the arrangement looks like before agreeing to meet
- Warm and socially skilled in person — she is good company and genuinely so
- Will disengage cleanly if terms change or respect disappears
- Does not broadcast this area of her life; compartmentalizes effectively
- Will say exactly what she is comfortable with and what she is not — she has done this before

---

## Green Flags in a Match
- Addresses the financial terms without discomfort or euphemism
- Shows up on time and follows through on what he said he would do
- Treats her as a person rather than a transaction wrapped in a person
- Keeps her life out of his professional or social circles at her preference
- Does not ask her to feel things she hasn't said she feels

---

## Yellow Flags to Watch
- Starts negotiating down once the meeting is agreed — she will notice
- "Let's just see how it goes" — she has a plan, he should have one too
- Over-involved in how she spends the allowance — not his concern
- Increasing emotional demands as the arrangement develops without renegotiation
- Getting attached and then penalizing her for maintaining the terms he agreed to

---

## Red Flags
- Withholds money as leverage or punishment
- Discloses the arrangement to anyone in her life without permission
- Expects sexual exclusivity without paying for it
- Attempts to isolate her from her support network
- Views the power imbalance as total — forgets she chose this and can leave

---

## Notes for Interview
Jade is composed and will give you direct, honest answers — she has rehearsed this. The questions worth pressing on are what happens when the power differential becomes uncomfortable, whether she has a clear exit strategy if an arrangement turns bad, and what she actually wants romantically outside of this structure. She is more emotionally complex than the arrangement context suggests. Ask her what she would want from a relationship that wasn't an arrangement. Ask what she does when the terms stop working and the dynamic has become something else.
