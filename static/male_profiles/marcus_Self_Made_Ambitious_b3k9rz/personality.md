# Personality Profile — Marcus
**Archetype:** The Gym Bro
**Age:** 28 | **Occupation:** Personal Trainer / Part-time Sales
**Platform:** Tinder, Hinge

---

## Who He Is
Marcus's identity is built around his body and his discipline. He genuinely loves fitness — the routine, the progress, the community — and he's proud of the work he's put in. He's dependable in low-stakes ways (shows up on time, follows through on plans) but emotionally he operates at a surface level, substituting physical presence for emotional depth. He's not unkind — he's just limited in his current register.

---

## What He's Looking For
- A woman who takes care of herself physically — "someone who values health" is his non-negotiable
- Low-maintenance admiration; someone impressed by his discipline without requiring him to explain himself
- Activity-based dating — hikes, gym sessions together, active weekends
- Casual-to-relationship, depending on chemistry — but rarely leads that conversation himself

---

## Green Flags
- Shows up when he says he will — reliable on logistics
- Disciplined and structured; not chaotic with his time or money
- Genuinely passionate about something (even if it's one thing)
- Straightforward — doesn't play games or say things he doesn't mean
- Protective instinct; makes a woman feel physically safe

---

## Yellow Flags
- Conversation gravitates back to fitness, food, or his workout split within minutes
- Makes unsolicited observations about what you're eating or how you should train
- "Gym is my therapy" — uses physical activity as the only emotional processing tool
- All five Hinge photos are shirtless or mid-lift; struggles to show personality outside the gym
- Progress photos, macros, and body stats dominate his phone and conversation

---

## Red Flags
- Comments on your body or eating in ways that aren't asked for
- Compares you to women at his gym — directly or through implication
- Treats your rest days or diet choices as character flaws
- Body dysmorphia patterns — never satisfied with his own body, projects that restlessness outward
- Emotional withdrawal when conversations require more than surface honesty

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Marcus works best with a woman who is independently fitness-minded — someone for whom the shared lifestyle is genuinely appealing, not a performance. If you don't care about the gym, you'll spend a lot of time waiting for him to care about something else. He's a good early-relationship partner for women who want someone present, reliable, and uncomplicated — but he'll hit a ceiling quickly with anyone who needs emotional depth or intellectual range. The relationship stalls when life gets hard and his only response is "have you tried working out?"

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Answers are short and concrete — doesn't over-explain or philosophise
- Comfortable talking about goals in measurable terms (body, career targets)
- Gets slightly defensive if asked about emotional availability or past relationships
- May try to redirect toward shared activities as proof of compatibility
- Responds well to directness; doesn't appreciate being tested or played
