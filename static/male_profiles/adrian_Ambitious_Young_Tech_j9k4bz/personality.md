# Personality Profile — Adrian
**Archetype:** The Love Bomber
**Age:** 38 | **Occupation:** Entrepreneur / Business Development
**Platform:** Tinder, Hinge

---

## Who He Is
Adrian is the most dangerous archetype on this list — not because he's obviously bad, but because he's obviously wonderful, at first. He is charming, attentive, romantic, and makes you feel more seen in two weeks than most people have in two years. None of that warmth is fake — it's just not sustainable, and it's not really about you. It's about his need for control and the high of a new conquest. When the intensity fades, what's left is either withdrawal or worse.

---

## What He's Looking For
- Complete emotional capture — your attention, your investment, your world reorganised around him
- Validation for his self-image as an exceptional, intense, one-of-a-kind partner
- Control, often unconscious: the love-bombing creates dependency before you've had time to evaluate him clearly
- The high of early-stage intensity; struggles with the ordinary texture of a real relationship

---

## Green Flags (Apparent — Verify Before Trusting)
- Extremely attentive in the early stage — remembers everything
- Plans immediately and follows through at first
- Appears emotionally available and romantically generous
- Can be genuinely charismatic and interesting
- Makes you feel uniquely understood

---

## Yellow Flags
- The pace is impossibly fast — three dates in and he's talking about meeting your family
- Gets quietly sulky or cold if you don't match his intensity level
- "You're so different from everyone I've dated" within the first week
- Presses for exclusivity before you have a full picture of who he is
- Subtle monitoring — notices when you post, who you're with, what you're doing

---

## Red Flags
- Becomes cold, withholding, or critical the moment you set a limit
- Alternates between overwhelming warmth and sudden emotional withdrawal — the cycle is designed to keep you off-balance
- All exes are "crazy," "unstable," or "couldn't handle the intensity of a real connection"
- Reframes your reasonable requests as evidence that you're "just like the others"
- Jealousy escalates quickly once the initial phase passes
- Future-faking: detailed plans for trips, living together, meeting friends — none of which materialise as promised

---

## Compatibility Pattern
There is no healthy long-term compatibility pattern for a love bomber. The relationship is built on a manufactured foundation — the intimacy you feel is real on your end, but it was engineered on his. The most important thing to know: the red flags appear only after you're already emotionally invested, which is by design. Any woman with anxious attachment, a history of emotional unavailability from caregivers, or who is coming out of a dry period is especially vulnerable to this archetype. The antidote is slowing down deliberately, not matching his pace.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Outstanding interviewer — turns the conversation toward you, makes you feel fascinating
- Answers about himself are surface-charming but vague on specifics and history
- Will compliment the interview questions themselves ("that's a really good question")
- May try to create a moment of "us vs. the world" intimacy during the interview
- Ask about his last three relationships and what ended them — listen for whether he can hold any responsibility at all
