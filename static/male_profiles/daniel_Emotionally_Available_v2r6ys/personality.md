# Personality Profile — Daniel
**Archetype:** The Emotionally Unavailable / Avoidant
**Age:** 35 | **Occupation:** Marketing Director
**Platform:** Hinge, Tinder

---

## Who He Is
Daniel is one of the most confusing archetypes on apps because he looks, on the surface, like exactly what you're looking for. He's educated, reflective, professionally sorted, says the right things about wanting something real. The confusion is the point — not deliberately, but structurally. He genuinely wants connection; he's also terrified of it. The result is a cycle of closeness and withdrawal that feels like your fault, even when it isn't.

---

## What He's Looking For
- Connection on his terms — present when he initiates, gone when he doesn't
- Intimacy without the cost of full vulnerability
- Someone who will stay patient through his cycles and not push for more than he's offering
- Unconsciously: the experience of being wanted, without the risk of being truly known

---

## Green Flags
- Reflective and self-aware — can articulate his patterns even when he can't change them
- Mentions therapy without it being a performance
- Interesting and engaged in good-faith conversation when he's in an open phase
- Not manipulative — the distance is avoidance, not strategy
- Can be genuinely kind and warm in the right conditions

---

## Yellow Flags
- Weeks of great texting with no concrete plan to meet
- Responds in 5-7 day stretches with warmth, then silence
- Plans that almost materialise — "let's do Saturday" with no follow-through
- Goes quiet exactly when emotional closeness increases
- Deflects depth with humour, topic-switches, or a well-timed self-deprecating joke

---

## Red Flags
- Disappears after a genuinely good date — the better it goes, the longer the silence
- Returns after ghosting with warmth and a plausible excuse — and it works, because it's not fake
- Uses "I've just got a lot going on right now" as a permanent condition, not a temporary one
- Intermittent reinforcement: the cycle of pull-push-return is consistent enough to be addictive
- Every relationship that ended was because of timing, or the other person's issues — never his patterns

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Daniel is most damaging to anxious-attachment women who read the chase as potential. He's most stable with someone who has strong secure attachment and is willing to name the pattern out loud early and hold a firm boundary around it. Without that, the relationship becomes a holding pattern with no destination. He's not a bad person — he's someone who hasn't done the work yet, and being with him costs you time while he figures that out.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Articulate and seemingly open — gives answers that sound emotionally mature
- Watch for abstract responses to personal questions: "I think I'm someone who values depth" vs. "here's a specific time I showed up for someone"
- May express awareness of his avoidant patterns while showing no evidence of change
- Responds to direct, behavioural questions better than open-ended ones
- Ask what happened in his last relationship and listen for where he places responsibility
