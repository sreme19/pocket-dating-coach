# Personality Profile — Alex
**Archetype:** The Monogamish Man
**Age:** 33 | **Occupation:** Journalist / Documentary Producer
**Platform:** Feeld, Hinge

---

## Who He Is
Alex is in, or is looking for, a primary relationship that is emotionally exclusive and the centre of his life. He is also honest about the fact that occasional shared sexual experiences — a threesome, a vetted third, a one-off with someone both partners agree on — are something he would want to explore with the right partner and the right trust in place. He does not use "monogamish" as a branding exercise: he has read the actual Dan Savage, has thought about what it means in practice, and understands that this requires more communication, not less, than conventional monogamy. He is not trying to get more than he said. He is trying to be honest about all of what he wants.

---

## What He's Looking For
- Stated: A serious, emotionally committed relationship where occasional shared experiences are possible by mutual agreement
- Actual: The same thing — he is unusually honest about this
- The gap: his experience with the actual practice is limited; the theory is sound, the execution is unproven

---

## Green Flags
- Can have the conversation about this without it becoming the entire conversation
- His primary interest is clearly the relationship; the sexual openness is additional, not foundational
- Can hear no for a specific situation without it becoming a tension in the primary bond
- Talks about consent and communication fluently because he has actually thought about them
- Has a life, close friendships, and emotional depth outside of this dimension

---

## Yellow Flags
- "Monogamish" is doing more work in his self-presentation than it should
- Has never actually navigated this in a relationship — all theory, no practice
- Brings it up early in a way that suggests it is more important to him than he frames it
- The pace at which he wants to explore it is faster than his stated "when we're ready"
- His last relationship ended partly because this caused friction he didn't anticipate

---

## Red Flags
- Uses the stated openness to push for situations his partner hasn't agreed to
- The primary relationship is not actually his priority — this is his priority inside a cover story
- Can't hear "I'm not interested in that" without withdrawing or becoming resentful
- His version of occasional becomes frequent without a conversation about the change
- Transparency applies when it benefits him; less consistently applied otherwise

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Alex is compatible with a partner who is genuinely and independently curious about occasional sexual openness within an otherwise committed relationship — someone who is not agreeing to make him happy, but wants this herself. The dynamic works when it is genuinely bilateral: both people are interested, both people set the pace, and the primary relationship is so secure that an occasional experience strengthens rather than tests it. It fails when one person is accommodating the other's preference and eventually runs out of accommodation.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Thoughtful and articulate — this is a journalist, he has words for things
- Ask what the relationship looks like when this specific dimension is off the table for a year
- Ask how he handled it the last time he wanted something his partner didn't
- Ask what he would do if the right situation arose and she said not yet
- Best question: "What would it mean to you if this was never part of the relationship?" — his genuine answer to that is the most diagnostic thing he can say
