# Personality Profile — Greg
**Archetype:** The Serial Dater / The Fixture
**Age:** 42 | **Occupation:** Regional Sales Manager
**Platform:** Hinge, Bumble, Match

---

## Who He Is
Greg has been on apps for seven years. His profile is essentially unchanged. He's been in four relationships since his mid-thirties, each lasting between three and six months, each ending — in his telling — because of circumstance, bad timing, or the other person's issues. He's not a bad person. He's socially skilled, stable, and excellent at the early phase of dating. What he cannot do is move past it. He's in love with courtship. The relationship itself makes him anxious in ways he has never examined.

---

## What He's Looking For
- The experience of early-stage dating — the energy, the discovery, the dopamine
- On paper: "ready to settle down" — this is what he believes about himself
- In practice: the cycle of meeting someone new every four to six months
- Someone patient enough that he can maintain the illusion of progression without actually arriving anywhere

---

## Green Flags
- Socially skilled and good at first dates — attentive, asks real questions, plans well
- Financially stable; treats dating seriously in the early stage
- Consistent communicator in the first month
- Has enough life experience to be interesting

---

## Yellow Flags
- Profile has been active for years — mutual connections have seen it throughout
- Pattern of 3-6 month relationships with vague explanations for why they ended
- The early-dating version of him is excellent; the long-term version is unavailable
- "I just haven't met the right person yet" as a permanent statement, not a temporary one
- Gets restless and distracted exactly when a relationship would move from exciting to real

---

## Red Flags
- Cannot articulate what he actually wants beyond "a real connection" and "someone genuine"
- Keeps multiple women in the "getting to know you" phase simultaneously
- His exits are always gentle and plausible — which makes them harder to see coming
- Has never been in a relationship that lasted more than a year as an adult
- The fading is so gradual it's almost impossible to point to a specific moment it started

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Greg is not compatible with anyone seeking genuine forward movement. He is compatible with women who also want companionship with warmth and no expectations — a mutually comfortable situationship with a relationship aesthetic. If you are looking for a partner, Greg will feel like the right person for exactly long enough to hurt when he exits. The tell is always the same: ask him what his longest relationship was, when it ended, and why.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Excellent interview performance — this is his strongest phase
- Answers about the future are warm but non-specific: "I want something real, I want to build something" — no timeline, no substance
- Well-practiced answers to common questions; can seem more self-aware than he is
- Ask specifically: "What's the longest relationship you've been in, and what ended it?" — the answer is always revealing
- Watch whether his answers about what he wants are consistent with his patterns when you press for specifics
