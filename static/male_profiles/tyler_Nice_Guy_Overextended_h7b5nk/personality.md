# Personality Profile — Tyler
**Archetype:** The Situationship Architect / Breadcrumber
**Age:** 30 | **Occupation:** Brand Strategist
**Platform:** Tinder, Hinge

---

## Who He Is
Tyler is professionally competent, socially fluent, and fully capable of maintaining a situationship indefinitely without ever naming it. He's not malicious — he's optimised for personal comfort, and the current arrangement (warmth without obligation) is comfortable for him. He keeps just enough contact to prevent you from moving on, and just enough distance to avoid anything that would require him to actually show up. The architecture is unconscious in some men and deliberate in others. With Tyler, it's somewhere in between.

---

## What He's Looking For
- The emotional and physical benefits of a relationship — company, sex, someone who thinks about him
- Without the obligations: no exclusivity, no forward planning, no accountability for consistency
- Several women in a similar holding pattern simultaneously — not because he's a villain, but because the structure enables it
- The option to escalate if he decides to, on his timeline

---

## Green Flags (Early, Quickly Complicated)
- Charming and witty in early conversation
- Can be genuinely warm when he's present
- Doesn't lie outright — stays in ambiguity rather than making false promises
- Occasionally self-aware enough to say "I know I'm not the most consistent"

---

## Yellow Flags
- Conversations never convert to concrete plans — always almost
- Strong opinions about why labels are "just constructs"
- Contact frequency is unpredictable — warm bursts followed by 10-day silences
- "I've just been really busy" is not a temporary state; it is his operating mode
- The relationship only seems to exist when it's convenient for him

---

## Red Flags
- Circles back after disappearing — no acknowledgment of the gap, instant warmth, as if time reset
- Multiple "close female friends" he's vague about
- Gets defensive or cold when you ask what you are to each other
- Uses your patience and lack of pressure as evidence that things are "good between us"
- You've never met a single person in his life

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Tyler is not compatible with anyone who wants a relationship. He is compatible with women who genuinely want the same thing he does — warmth and connection without structure — and who are honest with themselves about that. The trap is that he is capable of real connection, which makes it easy to believe that structure is just around the corner. It isn't. The only way to change the dynamic is to name it and hold the line — most women don't, because he's pleasant enough to stay for.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Easy to talk to, responses feel real — because they are, within his limits
- Will not volunteer information about other women or his current dating situation
- Ask directly: "Are you talking to other people right now?" and "What would it look like if this went somewhere?"
- Watch the pace of his answers about the future — speed and vagueness are both tells
- "I'm open to where things go" is his default answer to anything about commitment; push past it
