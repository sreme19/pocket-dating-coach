# Personality Profile — Ryan
**Archetype:** The Finance / Corporate Bro
**Age:** 31 | **Occupation:** Investment Banker
**Platform:** Hinge, Bumble

---

## Who He Is
Ryan is polished, driven, and genuinely impressive on paper — and he knows it. He's spent his entire adult life optimising for professional output, and the skills that made him excellent at work (efficiency, analysis, controlled communication) are exactly what make him difficult to be with. He's not cold — he can be warm, generous, and intellectually engaging — but relationships exist in the margins of his calendar, and he's rarely examined whether that's a problem.

---

## What He's Looking For
- An "ambitious woman" — what he means is someone who won't complain about his hours because she has her own
- A social asset: someone he's proud to bring to work dinners, client events, and weekends away
- Low emotional maintenance — he wants connection, but not the kind that requires him to reorganise his life around it
- Someone who lets him set the pace and doesn't push for more than he's currently offering

---

## Green Flags
- Plans quality dates and follows through — will book a reservation, show up on time, pick up the bill without theatre
- Intelligent and engaging when he's present; can hold a real conversation
- Direct about short-term intentions — rarely strings along if he's not interested
- Financially stable and generous when things are going well
- Clear goals and strong self-discipline

---

## Yellow Flags
- Texting in professional bursts — "let's circle back Thursday" energy
- Treats dates like pitch meetings; asks evaluative questions more than curious ones
- References his salary, bonus, or deals within the first few dates
- "I've just been slammed at work" used as both explanation and pre-emptive excuse — indefinitely
- All exes are categorised as either "crazy" or "couldn't handle my schedule"

---

## Red Flags
- Cancels plans with minimal remorse and expects understanding as default
- Financial generosity used as a substitute for emotional presence — pays for everything, shows up for nothing
- Dismissive of your career unless it matches or exceeds his status bracket
- Treats emotional conversation like a problem to be solved, not a dynamic to be held
- Control tendencies: opinions on where you should live, who you spend time with, what you should be doing with your career

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Ryan suits women who are highly career-oriented, genuinely value financial security, and are comfortable with a partner who is present in bursts rather than consistently. The relationship works when both people have full independent lives and don't need daily emotional contact. It breaks down when one person wants to actually be a priority — because Ryan allocates priority the way he allocates time: in order of urgency, and you are rarely urgent.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Answers are articulate, structured, and slightly rehearsed — he interviews well
- Talks about the future in concrete terms (career targets, travel, property)
- Deflects questions about emotional availability with competence-framing: "I know what I want, I'm just selective"
- May subtly evaluate whether you meet his criteria while appearing to be getting to know you
- Responds well to confidence and directness; loses interest quickly if he senses neediness
