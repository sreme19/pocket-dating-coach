# Personality Profile — Karan
**Archetype:** The Progressive-Traditional
**Age:** 34 | **Occupation:** Product Manager at a Tech Unicorn
**Platform:** Shaadi.com, Hinge, Bumble

---

## Who He Is
Karan is the most complex archetype in the Indian cohort because the contradiction is genuine — he's not performing progressiveness, he actually believes it. He grew up in a liberal urban household, studied and possibly lived abroad, has close female colleagues, and holds genuinely egalitarian views on most things. He also has deeply embedded traditional expectations about domestic life that he has never examined because they've never been challenged. The dissonance isn't conscious — which makes it harder to navigate than outright traditionalism.

---

## What He's Looking For
- Stated: "An independent, ambitious woman who has her own career. An equal partner."
- Actual: A credential-equal partner who will quietly accommodate his parents' expectations, his geography, and the domestic default — without it ever being framed as a compromise
- The gap between what he says and what he expects is real, and he experiences genuine confusion when you point it out

---

## Green Flags
- Educated, culturally interesting, financially stable
- More emotionally present than most South Asian archetypes in this cohort
- Genuinely believes in partnership — the progressive framing is not purely performative
- Interesting to talk to; has a worldview that's been tested by exposure
- Will show up for the relationship in ways that matter to him

---

## Yellow Flags
- "My family is very close-knit" in the first conversation — frequency of this phrase is a signal
- Mother's opinion matters significantly more than initially disclosed
- Progressive values apply to credentials and career; domestic defaults are unchallenged
- "She just couldn't handle my family involvement" framed as her failure, his norm
- Career relocation is always weighted toward his trajectory

---

## Red Flags
- Expects your professional ambitions to subordinate to his geography without negotiation
- Sunday family dinners are non-negotiable; your equivalent family time is "of course, just less often"
- Dowry expectations wrapped in indirect language about "family support" or "contributions"
- Increasing pressure around timeline that he framed as "whenever you're ready" until recently
- Emotional maturity applies to professional situations; reverts to family-script under relationship pressure

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Karan is compatible with ambitious, educated Indian women who want a culturally grounded partner and are genuinely comfortable navigating the stated-versus-actual gap — through strong communication and limit-setting from the beginning. The relationship has real potential when both parties are honest about the traditional defaults and decide together what to keep and what to renegotiate. It fails when he assumes accommodation and she assumes equality — both believing they agreed on the same thing.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Articulate and warm — presents as the most evolved version of himself
- Will say progressive things; ask him to give a specific recent example, not a principle
- Ask: "If your partner got a career opportunity in another city, what would actually happen?" — the answer is diagnostic
- Ask about his parents' role in his last relationship and how that affected it
- Best question: "What's something your family expects that you personally disagree with?" — how he handles that answer tells you everything
