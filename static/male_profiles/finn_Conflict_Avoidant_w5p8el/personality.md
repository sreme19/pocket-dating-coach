# Personality Profile — Finn
**Archetype:** The Softboy / Performative Sensitive
**Age:** 26 | **Occupation:** Graphic Designer / Musician
**Platform:** Hinge

---

## Who He Is
Finn is the fuckboy who read enough to disguise himself. He has the emotional vocabulary, the cultural references, the thoughtful opener, the Spotify playlist he'll share by day three. He's genuinely interesting and the connection feels real — because in the moment, it often is. What's missing is the follow-through. The sensitivity is a real trait that he has not developed into a relational skill. He knows how to seem emotionally available. He does not know how to be it.

---

## What He's Looking For
- The emotional labour of a relationship — deep conversation, feeling understood, being desired
- Without the structure — no exclusivity talks, no future planning, no accountability
- Someone who finds him fascinating enough to stay through the inconsistency
- Creative validation: someone who engages with his work and treats his art as important

---

## Green Flags
- Genuinely stimulating to talk to — witty, warm, references things worth referencing
- Remembers what you said and asks follow-up questions
- Makes you feel like the most interesting person in the room when he's engaged
- Emotionally literate vocabulary — can name feelings, notice patterns, discuss dynamics
- Creative talent is often real; not just performance

---

## Yellow Flags
- Impeccably curated profile — too polished to be spontaneous
- "I don't really believe in labels" early in the conversation
- Intellectualises feelings rather than expressing them directly
- "I'm really focused on my art right now" as a recurring explanation for availability
- The pace slows almost imperceptibly once real feelings start developing on your end

---

## Red Flags
- Disappears after the first moment of genuine physical or emotional intimacy
- Reappears weeks later as if nothing happened — warm, curious, completely unreferencing the gap
- Uses emotional awareness as a seduction tool, not as an actual commitment to showing up
- Never initiates a "what are we" conversation — and deflects it every time you do
- The cycle repeats: closeness, withdrawal, reappearance, closeness — indefinitely until you end it

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Finn suits women who are genuinely secure and unbothered by ambiguity — who can enjoy the intellectual and emotional stimulation of his company without needing it to convert into something defined. For anyone who needs consistency or progression, he will be a beautiful, frustrating waste of time. The honest version of Finn, when he eventually does the work, can become an excellent partner — but that's a future version, not a present one.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Best interviewer experience of any archetype — warm, curious, makes it feel like a real conversation
- Answers personal questions with insight but keeps moving before you can probe deeper
- Will pivot to your experience when his gets uncomfortable: "but what about you — how do you see it?"
- Ask directly: "what does commitment mean to you right now?" — watch whether he gives a real answer or a beautiful non-answer
- Follow up on anything vague; he'll fill silence with charm if you let him
