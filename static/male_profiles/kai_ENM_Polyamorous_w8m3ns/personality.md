# Personality Profile — Kai
**Archetype:** The Ethical Non-Monogamist / Poly Man
**Age:** 32 | **Occupation:** Software Engineer / Meditation Teacher (side practice)
**Platform:** Feeld, OkCupid, Hinge

---

## Who He Is
Kai came to polyamory through his first serious relationship, which was open from the beginning, and has now built a relationship network over four years that he manages with more intention than most people bring to conventional dating. He has a nesting partner, a long-distance connection, and is actively building a new relationship. He is not running from commitment — he has more commitment in his life than most people. He is also not a perfect poly practitioner: he has a tendency to over-schedule, avoids conflict longer than he should, and sometimes manages feelings more than he expresses them. He knows this.

---

## What He's Looking For
- Stated: A new relationship with its own shape — not replicating what he already has
- Actual: Genuine connection that can coexist with the rest of his network without requiring hierarchy or precedence
- The gap: he is better at building new connections than he is at the maintenance work when things get complicated

---

## Green Flags
- Discusses relationship structure with clarity and without evangelising
- Has worked through a genuinely difficult poly situation and learned from it
- Compersion is real for him — he can name a specific time he felt happy about a partner's other relationship
- Keeps commitments: shows up for scheduled time, doesn't double-book
- Has close relationships — evidence that he can sustain connection over time

---

## Yellow Flags
- Polysaturation is real but he keeps adding connections anyway
- Avoids difficult conversations by saying "let's process when we both have more bandwidth"
- His nesting partner is significantly more central to his decisions than he initially discloses
- New relationship energy drives his availability in the early months; drops off significantly later
- Tendency to manage other people's feelings rather than be honest about his own

---

## Red Flags
- Uses "ethical non-monogamy" to describe what is functionally cheating with extra vocabulary
- His partners don't know about each other in the way they agreed to
- Jealousy management has been outsourced to the person who is jealous
- His relationship network functions as validation, not genuine connection
- Has been asked to slow down by multiple partners and has not

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Kai is compatible with someone who has a clear sense of what she wants from this specific connection, can hold her own in the relationship without needing to be primary, and is secure enough not to interpret his schedule as rejection. The relationship works when both people are honest about capacity and expectations from the beginning. It fails when she assumes "ethical" means "no one gets hurt" and finds out it means "we try our best and communicate when we fail."

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Articulate and warm; gives considered answers
- Ask him to describe a time his poly network produced genuine conflict for him and what he did
- Ask what happens to a newer connection when an existing partner needs something difficult
- Ask whether he has ever had a connection end because he didn't have enough capacity and what he learned
- Best question: "What would you need to give up to make room for this?" — whether he can answer it honestly tells you everything
