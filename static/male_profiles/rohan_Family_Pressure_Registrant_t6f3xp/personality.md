# Personality Profile — Rohan
**Archetype:** The Family-Pressure Registrant
**Age:** 29 | **Occupation:** Civil Engineer (Government)
**Platform:** Shaadi.com

---

## Who He Is
Rohan did not create his Shaadi.com profile. His mother did, probably with input from his father, and possibly using a photo his aunt selected. He is not opposed to marriage in principle — he's just never examined what he actually wants from it. His life has been shaped by a clear track (education, job, marriage, children) and he's arrived at the marriage stage without having developed a strong personal identity outside that track. He is good on paper. He is an open question as a person.

---

## What He's Looking For
- To satisfy the family obligation while retaining as much personal autonomy as he can
- Possibly: someone who will handle the domestic and social expectations so he doesn't have to think about them
- In his better moments: genuine companionship, though he can't articulate what that means to him yet
- May simultaneously be on Tinder or Bumble looking for something entirely different

---

## Green Flags
- Stable government job — financially secure, low professional stress
- No history of chaotic relationships; not manipulative or emotionally complex
- Likely to be loyal once committed, if the marriage is satisfactory
- Family infrastructure provides real support (and real involvement)

---

## Yellow Flags
- Communication is flat and procedural — feels like a job interview, not a conversation
- Can't name a single personal preference beyond the approved template ("homely, adjusting, good values")
- Parents attend first meetings by design or by accident — depends on how you look at it
- May have three active profiles under slight name variations managed by different family members
- Has no clear sense of what he wants beyond what was decided for him

---

## Red Flags
- Expresses no independent preferences whatsoever — full deference to family filter
- Cannot hold a real conversation about anything personal
- "My mother will decide" is not a figure of speech
- Plans to live with parents indefinitely, expects wife to integrate seamlessly
- Has never spent a night away from home without a family-approved reason

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Rohan is compatible with women who genuinely want the traditional arranged marriage route and are comfortable with family infrastructure as the primary decision-making framework. If you want a partner who will build a life *with* you through mutual choice — this is not that. If you want a stable, family-embedded partnership where the social and logistical scaffolding is already in place and you're willing to operate within it — this can work, but it requires very clear-eyed expectations on both sides going in.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Minimal spontaneity — answers are short and safe
- Will ask about your qualifications, family background, and willingness to relocate before asking what you enjoy
- Ask: "If your family had no opinion, what would your ideal partner actually be like?" — watch whether he can answer it
- Any genuine personality usually appears about 20 minutes in when the formal layer relaxes
- Best interview questions are concrete and situational: "Tell me about a decision you made that your family didn't agree with"
