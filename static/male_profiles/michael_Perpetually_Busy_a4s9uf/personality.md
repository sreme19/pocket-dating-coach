# Personality Profile — Michael
**Archetype:** The Recently Divorced
**Age:** 44 | **Occupation:** Architect
**Platform:** Bumble, Hinge

---

## Who He Is
Michael is a fundamentally decent person navigating one of the hardest transitions an adult goes through — and he is at various stages of doing it well or poorly depending on when you meet him. He has real-life depth: he's built things, raised children, survived a marriage falling apart. The question is never whether he's a good person. It's whether he's ready — and the answer depends heavily on which version of him you're encountering.

**Three sub-types to distinguish:**
- **The Rebounder:** Flooded with energy and emotion, moves extremely fast, exits once the fog clears. Genuine but not ready.
- **The Oversharer:** Processes his divorce out loud, on your time. Every date doubles as a therapy session about his ex.
- **The Ready One:** Has genuinely done the work, knows what went wrong, is clear about what he wants next. Rare. Real.

---

## What He's Looking For
- Rebounder: Intimacy and validation, fast. Not a real relationship yet, even if he believes it is.
- Oversharer: Someone to help him process. Needs a therapist, not a girlfriend.
- The Ready One: A real partner. Knows what cohabitation looks like. Wants to build again, not just heal.

---

## Green Flags
- Life experience and self-knowledge (if processed)
- Knows what he wants domestically — has actually lived with a partner
- Financially stable and clear about his situation
- Protective and considerate — has learned from the marriage, good or bad
- Present and attentive in a way that younger men often aren't

---

## Yellow Flags
- First time on apps — learning the norms in real time
- Kids complicate availability in ways he may understate early on
- Divorce not yet finalised — legally and emotionally a different situation
- Intensity of early interest is hard to read: genuine or rebound?
- Ex-wife appears in conversations more than the situation warrants

---

## Red Flags
- Mentions ex-wife in the first three dates without her being directly relevant
- Moves to exclusivity talk within two weeks of meeting
- Unresolved anger or contempt toward the ex — contempt especially
- Kids are used as a reason everything is complicated, including showing up for you
- Idealises you as the solution to his pain rather than as a person

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Michael (the Ready One version) is compatible with women 35+ who want a relationship with real substance and aren't building from scratch — someone who values stability, has her own full life, and isn't intimidated by the complexity of a partner with kids. He's a poor fit for women in their 20s who want to build together from the beginning. The Rebounder and Oversharer versions are poor fits for anyone — the kindest thing you can do for them is be honest about what you're seeing.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- More self-reflective than most archetypes — has genuinely thought about his patterns
- Will reference his kids early; notice whether it's pride or excuse
- Ask directly: "What did the end of your marriage teach you about yourself?" — the answer tells you which version of Michael you're talking to
- Rebounder: answer will be about what his ex did. Ready One: answer will include what he did.
- Watch for ex-wife mentions that arrive without prompting — frequency is diagnostic
