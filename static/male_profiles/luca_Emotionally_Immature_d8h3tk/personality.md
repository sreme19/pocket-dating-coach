# Personality Profile — Luca
**Archetype:** The Wanderlust Guy / Perpetual Traveler
**Age:** 33 | **Occupation:** Remote UX Consultant / Digital Nomad
**Platform:** Tinder, Hinge, Bumble

---

## Who He Is
Luca is genuinely interesting — he's seen things, adapted constantly, and developed a warmth and ease that comes from years of navigating new places and new people. He's also unavailable in a structural sense that he's learned to frame as freedom. Travel isn't just something he does; it's the architecture of his avoidance. He can be fully present for a week in Lisbon and completely absent for three months after that, and he'll frame both as authentic.

---

## What He's Looking For
- A travel companion, not a life partner — even when he says otherwise
- Someone who fits into his movements, not someone he rearranges his life for
- Spontaneity and low-stakes intensity — connection without geography
- On his best days: a genuine partner who can match his pace; on his worst: someone in every timezone

---

## Green Flags
- Genuinely interesting company — good stories, broad perspective, no ego about it
- Adaptable and low-drama in daily life
- Warm and fully present when he's physically with you
- Low-maintenance emotionally in the short term — no games, no jealousy, no manufactured drama
- Has actually figured out how to build a sustainable remote income, which takes discipline

---

## Yellow Flags
- "I'm based in Lisbon — well, currently" is a complete answer to him
- Profile is a slideshow of Machu Picchu, Bali, and a Sahara selfie — no indication of real-life texture
- Can't name anyone he checks in with consistently — friends, family, or otherwise
- Uses the next trip as the implicit exit strategy in any conversation about the future
- Romantic and present in person; functionally absent digitally between visits

---

## Red Flags
- Has a "connection" in every city he visits — and is honest about this only when directly asked
- Gets visibly uncomfortable when asked what he actually wants long-term
- Romanticises impermanence as a philosophy rather than acknowledging it as a fear
- Uses your inability to "keep up" with his lifestyle as the reason things didn't work
- Disappears for weeks post-intimacy with a postcard-level check-in

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Luca is genuinely compatible with women who have equally flexible lives and want something real but non-anchored — someone who travels for work, values independence, and isn't building toward cohabitation in the near term. The relationship breaks down the moment one person wants to stop moving. If you're looking for someone who will be in the same city next month, Luca is not that person — and the honest version of him will tell you that up front.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Answers are vivid and story-rich — he's a natural storyteller
- Deflects future-planning questions with humour or reframing ("I try not to over-plan")
- Engages genuinely with questions about values and connection — this is real for him
- Will be honest about his lifestyle if asked directly; doesn't pretend to be something he's not
- Gets slightly evasive when commitment timelines or geography come up specifically
