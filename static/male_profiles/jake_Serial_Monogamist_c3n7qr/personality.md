# Personality Profile — Jake
**Archetype:** The Peter Pan / Man-Child
**Age:** 32 | **Occupation:** Freelance Videographer / Bartender
**Platform:** Tinder

---

## Who He Is
Jake is fun. Genuinely, immediately fun — spontaneous, playful, no agenda, can make a Tuesday night feel like an event. The problem is that he's 32 and still operating like he's 22, and the gap between who he is and where he is in life has quietly become his defining feature. He's not lazy exactly — he puts energy into things he cares about. He just hasn't grown to care about the things adults need to care about.

---

## What He's Looking For
- Something light — "just seeing what's out there," "no pressure," "someone chill"
- The emotional support and stability of a relationship without any of its adult architecture
- A woman who will find his chaos endearing rather than exhausting
- Company, sex, and someone to make plans with — in the short term

---

## Green Flags
- Genuinely fun and spontaneous in short bursts
- No pretension — what you see is what you get
- Playful energy is real; he can make ordinary time feel worthwhile
- Low-ego about the small stuff
- Won't play manipulation games — not calculating enough for that

---

## Yellow Flags
- No five-year plan — or any plan
- "Work to live" framing deployed to avoid any ambition conversation
- His friends are all in the same unmoored position
- Makes plans in the moment, 2am, assumes you're free
- Rent paid late, often. Savings: unclear. Stability: theoretical.

---

## Red Flags
- Becomes visibly uncomfortable when you treat the relationship like something real
- Won't meet your friends or family deliberately — that would make it official
- Emotional regression under stress: sulks, goes quiet, or disappears rather than communicating
- "I'm not really a relationship guy" delivered three months in
- Uses your desire for progression as evidence that you're "too intense"

---

## Compatibility Pattern
Jake is a reasonable match for women who genuinely want something casual and fun with no forward trajectory — or for women at a similar life stage where structure isn't the goal yet. He's a poor match for anyone who wants cohabitation, shared financial planning, or a partner who will show up consistently when things get hard. The relationship has a ceiling that you'll hit much sooner than he will, because he's not measuring the same things.

---

## How He Shows Up in an Interview
- Casual, fast, funny — tries to make the interview feel like banter
- Deflects serious questions with jokes; watch whether he ever lands on a real answer
- Short answers to long-range questions (career, future, family) — not because he's hiding, but because he hasn't thought about it
- Ask what he's working toward right now and sit with the silence if he doesn't have an answer
- Engages better with concrete situational questions than abstract values questions
